## Executive Summary

This document presents a capability gap analysis for Quantum Energy, comparing our current state maturity with our target state maturity levels for a selection of critical business capabilities. The analysis identifies the delta, or "gap," that needs to be addressed through strategic initiatives and focused investment. This informs our transformation roadmap, ensuring resources are directed to capabilities most critical for achieving Quantum Energy's strategic objectives across both regulated and non-regulated business segments.

---

## 1. Methodology

The gap analysis is performed by comparing the "Current Maturity Level" (`capability-maturity-current.md`) against the "Target Maturity Level" (`capability-maturity-target.md`) for each selected capability. The "Gap" represents the number of maturity levels that need to be advanced. A higher gap indicates a more significant transformation effort is required.

## 3. Key Observations & Transformation Priorities

*   **Significant Digitalization Gaps:** Capabilities related to Digital Customer Experience (10.5) and Customer Insights & Analytics (10.8) show a 2-level gap. Closing these gaps is paramount for Quantum Energy's enterprise-wide customer-centricity objectives and to support the data-driven growth of non-regulated offerings.
*   **Non-Regulated Growth Dependencies:** Both `Product Development & Engineering` (11.3) and `Customer Acquisition & Conversion` (12.4) in the non-regulated space have a 2-level gap. This indicates a need for substantial investment in processes, tools, and talent to rapidly scale our non-regulated business and meet revenue targets.
*   **Regulated Modernization:** `Distribution Automation & Grid Modernization` (8.5) is a critical 2-level gap that requires sustained investment to meet regulated reliability standards and enable advanced grid functions like DER integration.
*   **Continuous Improvement for Leaders:** Even highly mature capabilities like `Electric System Balancing & Dispatch` (13.3) have a 1-level gap, indicating a commitment to continuous optimization and pushing the boundaries of operational excellence.
*   **Enabling Functions:** Capabilities like `Cybersecurity & Information Security` (16.4) have a 1-level gap but are assigned high priority due to their foundational importance to the entire enterprise's security posture and compliance.
