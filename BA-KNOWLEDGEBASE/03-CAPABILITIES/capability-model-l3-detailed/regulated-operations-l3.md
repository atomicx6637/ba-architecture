## 2. Electric Distribution L3 Capabilities

**Parent L1 Capability:** 8. Electric Distribution (Reg)
**Definition:** The capability to deliver electrical energy from the transmission system to end-use customers through medium and low-voltage distribution infrastructure.

### 2.1. Parent L2 Capability: 8.2. Distribution System Maintenance & Repair

**Definition:** The capability to maintain and repair overhead and underground distribution lines, substations, and associated equipment.

#### L3 Capabilities:

*   **8.2.1. Overhead Line Maintenance:** Perform routine inspection, repair, and replacement of overhead conductors, poles, crossarms, and associated hardware.
*   **8.2.2. Underground Cable Maintenance:** Conduct inspection, repair, and replacement of underground primary and secondary cables, conduits, and vaults.
*   **8.2.3. Distribution Substation Maintenance:** Perform preventive and corrective maintenance on distribution transformers, switchgear, protective relays, and control equipment.
*   **8.2.4. Fault Location & Identification:** Detect and precisely locate faults on the distribution system using specialized equipment and analytical tools.
*   **8.2.5. Emergency Repair & Restoration:** Execute rapid repair and restoration activities following outages caused by equipment failure, storms, or other emergencies.
*   **8.2.6. Asset Health Monitoring & Diagnostics:** Utilize sensor data and diagnostic tools to assess the condition and predict potential failures of distribution assets.

### 2.2. Parent L2 Capability: 8.6. Outage Management & Restoration

**Definition:** The capability to detect, respond to, and restore electric service during outages and system disturbances.

#### L3 Capabilities:

*   **8.6.1. Outage Detection & Verification:** Identify and verify power outages through AMI data, SCADA alarms, customer calls, and field reports.
*   **8.6.2. Customer Outage Communication:** Provide proactive and reactive communication to customers regarding outage status, estimated restoration times (ETR), and safety information.
*   **8.6.3. Crew Dispatch & Mobilization:** Allocate and dispatch field crews and resources efficiently to outage locations based on priority and availability.
*   **8.6.4. Damage Assessment & Prioritization:** Assess the extent of damage at outage sites and prioritize restoration efforts based on safety, impact, and system conditions.
*   **8.6.5. Restoration Execution:** Perform necessary repairs, switching operations, and testing to safely restore power to affected customers.
*   **8.6.6. Outage Reporting & Analysis:** Document outage events, track restoration metrics (SAIDI, SAIFI), and conduct post-event analysis for continuous improvement.

## 3. Gas Distribution L3 Capabilities

**Parent L1 Capability:** 9. Gas Distribution (Reg)
**Definition:** The capability to deliver natural gas from transmission pipelines to end-use customers through low-pressure distribution infrastructure.

### 3.1. Parent L2 Capability: 9.5. Leak Detection & Pipeline Safety

**Definition:** The capability to detect, locate, and repair gas leaks, and to maintain the integrity and safety of the gas distribution network.

#### L3 Capabilities:

*   **9.5.1. Leak Survey & Detection:** Conduct systematic surveys (e.g., walking, mobile, aerial) to detect gas leaks using specialized equipment.
*   **9.5.2. Leak Classification & Prioritization:** Classify detected leaks based on severity (e.g., Grade 1, 2, 3) and prioritize repair activities according to safety protocols.
*   **9.5.3. Leak Location & Pinpointing:** Precisely locate the source of identified leaks for targeted repair efforts.
*   **9.5.4. Leak Repair & Remediation:** Execute repairs on gas mains, service lines, and associated equipment to eliminate leaks and restore system integrity.
*   **9.5.5. Public Awareness Programs (Damage Prevention):** Implement programs to educate the public and excavators about safe digging practices (e.g., 811/One Call system) to prevent third-party damage.
*   **9.5.6. PHMSA Compliance Auditing:** Conduct internal audits and prepare for external inspections to ensure full compliance with PHMSA safety regulations.

### 3.2. Parent L2 Capability: 9.6. Pressure Regulation & Monitoring

**Definition:** The capability to regulate and monitor gas pressure throughout the distribution system to ensure safe and reliable delivery.

#### L3 Capabilities:

*   **9.6.1. Regulator Station Operations:** Operate and maintain gas pressure regulator stations that reduce gas pressure from transmission levels to distribution levels.
*   **9.6.2. Service Regulator Operations:** Inspect, maintain, and replace service regulators located at customer premises to ensure safe delivery pressure.
*   **9.6.3. System Pressure Monitoring:** Continuously monitor gas pressure at various points across the distribution network using SCADA and telemetry.
*   **9.6.4. Overpressure Protection Management:** Design, install, and maintain overpressure protection devices (e.g., relief valves, shut-off valves) to prevent unsafe pressure conditions.
*   **9.6.5. Pressure Zone Optimization:** Analyze and optimize gas pressure zones within the distribution system to ensure efficient and reliable service while minimizing leakage.
*   **9.6.6. Pressure Incident Response:** Respond to and manage incidents involving abnormal gas pressure conditions (e.g., overpressure, underpressure) to ensure public safety and system integrity.
