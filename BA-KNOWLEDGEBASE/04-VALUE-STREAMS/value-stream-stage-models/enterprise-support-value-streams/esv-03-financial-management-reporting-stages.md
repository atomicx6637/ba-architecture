## Executive Summary

This document details the stages of the Financial Management & Reporting value stream (ESV-03). This value stream describes the end-to-end processes for managing the company's finances, from recording daily transactions to producing official financial statements for internal and external use. It is the financial backbone of the corporation, ensuring fiscal discipline, regulatory compliance, and transparency to investors and other stakeholders.

---

## 1. Value Stream Context

**Value Stream ID:** ESV-03
**Name:** Financial Management & Reporting
**Description:** Manage financial planning, accounting, cost allocation, and reporting for the entire enterprise, ensuring financial integrity, transparency, and compliance.

**Entry Criteria:**
-   A business transaction with a financial impact occurs (e.g., a purchase, a sale, payroll).
-   A financial period (e.g., month, quarter, year) is initiated.
-   A request for a budget or forecast is made.

**Exit Criteria:**
-   Official financial statements (internal and external) are produced and published.
-   All regulatory financial reports are filed accurately and on time.
-   Internal management has the financial reports and analysis needed to make business decisions.
-   Adherence to budgets is monitored and managed.

## 3. Cross-Value Stream Dependencies

*   **ESV-03** is the "scorekeeper" for all other value streams, as every business activity ultimately has a financial impact that must be recorded and reported here.
*   It is inextricably linked with **ESV-01 Enterprise Strategic Planning & Execution**, providing the financial analysis to support strategy and managing the budget that funds it.
*   It works with **REV-01 Rate Case & Cost Recovery** to provide the audited financial data needed for regulatory filings.
*   It manages the payroll processed as part of **ESV-02 Talent Management** and the procurement spend from **ESV-06 Procurement & Supply Chain Management**.
