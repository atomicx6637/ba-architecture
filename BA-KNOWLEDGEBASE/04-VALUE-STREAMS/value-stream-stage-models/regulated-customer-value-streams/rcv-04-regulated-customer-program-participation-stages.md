## Executive Summary

This document details the stages of the Regulated Customer Program Participation value stream (RCV-04). This process describes how Quantum Energy markets, enrolls, manages, and measures the impact of regulated programs such as energy efficiency, demand response, and low-income assistance. Successful execution of this value stream is key to meeting regulatory mandates, managing system load, and providing additional value to customers.

---

## 1. Value Stream Context

**Value Stream ID:** RCV-04
**Name:** Regulated Customer Program Participation
**Description:** Enable regulated customers to enroll in and participate in energy efficiency, demand response, or other utility-mandated programs.

**Entry Criteria:**
-   A regulated program is approved, funded, and open for enrollment.
-   A customer meets the specific eligibility criteria for a program.
-   A customer expresses interest or responds to a targeted program offer.

**Exit Criteria:**
-   The customer is successfully enrolled and actively participating in the program.
-   Program benefits (e.g., rebates, bill credits, incentives) are delivered to the customer.
-   Program performance and impacts (e.g., kWh saved, kW shed) are measured, verified, and reported.

## 3. Cross-Value Stream Dependencies

*   **RCV-04** enrollment and marketing is often managed through interactions within **RCV-02 Regulated Customer Service Delivery**.
*   Benefits are often delivered through modifications in **RCV-03 Regulated Billing & Payment**.
*   Demand Response programs within **RCV-04** are a critical, non-wires alternative resource for **AOV-06 System Reliability & Emergency Response**.
*   The overall program budget and goals are influenced by **REV-01 Rate Case & Cost Recovery** and **REV-02 Regulatory Compliance & Reporting**.
