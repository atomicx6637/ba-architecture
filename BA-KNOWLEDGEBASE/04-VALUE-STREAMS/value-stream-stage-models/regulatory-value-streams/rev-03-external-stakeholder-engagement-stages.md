## Executive Summary

This document details the stages of the External Stakeholder Engagement value stream (REV-03). This process describes how Quantum Energy proactively and reactively engages with a wide range of non-customer, non-regulator stakeholders, including community groups, local governments, policymakers, media, and investors. Building and maintaining these relationships is crucial for maintaining our social license to operate, managing reputation, and advancing strategic goals.

---

## 1. Value Stream Context

**Value Stream ID:** REV-03
**Name:** External Stakeholder Engagement
**Description:** Engage with communities, local governments, policymakers, and other external stakeholders to build trust, address concerns, and influence energy policy.

**Entry Criteria:**
-   An external stakeholder concern, inquiry, or engagement opportunity is identified.
-   A major project or initiative requiring public outreach is launched (e.g., new power line, plant decommissioning).
-   A proactive engagement strategy for a specific stakeholder group is initiated.

**Exit Criteria:**
-   Stakeholder concerns are understood, acknowledged, and addressed where appropriate.
-   Trust and constructive relationships are strengthened.
-   Community, political, or investor support for company initiatives is maintained or improved.
-   Corporate and brand reputation is protected or enhanced.

## 3. Cross-Value Stream Dependencies

*   **REV-03** is critical during **AOV-02 Capital Project Delivery** for large projects that impact communities.
*   It works hand-in-hand with **AOV-06 System Reliability & Emergency Response** to manage public and media communications during major outages.
*   This value stream supports the objectives of **REV-01 Rate Case & Cost Recovery** by building political and community support.
*   It is closely linked to **ESV-01 Enterprise Strategic Planning & Execution** as stakeholder sentiment is a key input to strategy.
