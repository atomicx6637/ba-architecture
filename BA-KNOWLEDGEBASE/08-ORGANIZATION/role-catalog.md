## Executive Summary

This document provides a high-level catalog of key roles within Quantum Energy. It outlines their primary responsibilities and how they contribute to the execution of business capabilities and value streams across both our regulated utility operations and non-regulated advanced energy solutions. This catalog serves as a foundational reference for clarifying accountabilities, informing workforce planning, and designing effective organizational processes.

---

## 1. Introduction

Roles define the functions and responsibilities that individuals or teams perform within an organization. Quantum Energy's roles are designed to support our operating model, balancing specialized expertise for regulated functions with agility for non-regulated ventures, and efficiency for shared services.

## 2. Executive Leadership Roles

### 2.1. Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Provides overall strategic leadership and direction for Quantum Energy. Accountable for the company's financial performance, operational excellence, and stakeholder relations across both regulated and non-regulated entities.
*   **Key Contribution:** Drives strategic vision, sets corporate goals, manages investor relations, ensures regulatory compliance and non-regulated growth.

### 2.2. Chief Operating Officer (COO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Accountable for the safe, reliable, and efficient operation of all regulated electric and natural gas infrastructure (generation, transmission, distribution, supply/storage). Oversees operational compliance and asset management.
*   **Key Contribution:** Ensures operational excellence, drives grid modernization, manages capital projects for regulated assets, maintains public safety and reliability standards.

### 2.3. Chief Digital & Innovation Officer (CDIO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Leads the Advanced Energy Solutions (AES) business unit. Accountable for the strategy, development, sales, marketing, and delivery of all non-regulated products and services. Drives digital transformation initiatives across the enterprise.
*   **Key Contribution:** Fosters innovation, develops new revenue streams, expands non-regulated market share, leverages digital technologies for enterprise-wide efficiency.

### 2.4. Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Accountable for Quantum Energy's financial planning, accounting, treasury, and investor relations functions. Ensures financial integrity, prudent capital allocation, and effective financial risk management for the entire enterprise.
*   **Key Contribution:** Manages regulated cost recovery, optimizes non-regulated profitability, ensures financial transparency and compliance, maintains strong credit ratings.

### 2.5. Chief Customer Officer (CCO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Accountable for the overall customer experience strategy and execution across all regulated and non-regulated customer interactions. Oversees customer service, customer insights, and corporate communications functions.
*   **Key Contribution:** Drives customer satisfaction, develops unified customer journeys, manages brand reputation, leads corporate social responsibility initiatives.

### 2.6. Chief Information Officer (CIO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Accountable for the strategy, development, and operation of Quantum Energy's enterprise-wide information technology and data management capabilities. Ensures cybersecurity and data integrity.
*   **Key Contribution:** Provides secure and reliable IT infrastructure, enables digital transformation, leverages data analytics for business insights, supports regulated OT and non-regulated platforms.

### 2.7. Chief Strategy Officer (CSO)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Accountable for the development, communication, and execution monitoring of Quantum Energy's overall corporate strategy. Leads enterprise planning, market intelligence, and corporate business development initiatives.
*   **Key Contribution:** Translates vision into actionable plans, identifies strategic opportunities (regulated and non-regulated), ensures cross-functional strategic alignment.

## 3. Key Functional/Business Unit Roles

### 3.1. Vice President, Electric Distribution

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Manages the safe, reliable, and efficient operation, maintenance, and planning of Quantum Energy's regulated electric distribution network.
*   **Key Contribution:** Oversees grid modernization initiatives, manages outage response, ensures service connections, leads asset management for distribution assets.

### 3.2. Vice President, Natural Gas Distribution

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Manages the safe, reliable, and efficient operation, maintenance, and planning of Quantum Energy's regulated natural gas distribution network.
*   **Key Contribution:** Oversees pipeline safety, manages leak detection and repair, ensures service connections, leads asset management for gas distribution assets.

### 3.3. VP, Product Development - AES (Non-Regulated)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Leads the end-to-end lifecycle of non-regulated product development, from concept ideation and design to engineering, launch, and ongoing management.
*   **Key Contribution:** Identifies market needs, develops innovative solutions (smart home, EV, DG), ensures product-market fit, drives product profitability.

### 3.4. VP, Sales & Marketing - AES (Non-Regulated)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Leads all sales and marketing activities for Quantum Energy's non-regulated products and services. Accountable for customer acquisition, revenue growth, and brand presence in competitive markets.
*   **Key Contribution:** Develops go-to-market strategies, manages sales channels, executes marketing campaigns, fosters customer loyalty for non-regulated offerings.

### 3.5. Head of Enterprise Architecture

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Defines and governs Quantum Energy's enterprise architecture landscape (business, data, application, technology). Leads the Business Architecture practice.
*   **Key Contribution:** Ensures strategic alignment of all architectural domains, drives architectural standards, facilitates cross-functional architectural planning, optimizes IT-business alignment.

### 3.6. Data Scientist / Analyst

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Analyzes complex datasets (operational, customer, market) to identify trends, create predictive models, and generate actionable insights for both regulated optimization and non-regulated product development.
*   **Key Contribution:** Drives data-driven decision-making, supports personalized customer experiences, enhances operational efficiency, identifies new business opportunities.

### 3.7. Field Operations Technician (Regulated)

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Executes the safe and efficient installation, maintenance, and repair of regulated electric and natural gas infrastructure. Responds to emergencies and outages.
*   **Key Contribution:** Ensures system reliability, maintains public safety, delivers physical service, interacts directly with customers in the field.

### 3.8. Non-Regulated Solution Installer

*   **Primary Responsibility:** Performs professional installation and activation of non-regulated products (e.g., smart home devices, EV chargers, solar panels) at customer premises. Provides initial customer onboarding and technical support.
*   **Key Contribution:** Ensures high-quality product delivery, drives customer satisfaction with non-regulated offerings, acts as a brand ambassador.

## 4. Role Design Principles

*   **Clear Accountability:** Each role has well-defined responsibilities and reporting lines.
*   **Empowerment:** Roles are designed to empower individuals to make decisions appropriate to their level of responsibility.
*   **Flexibility:** Roles and teams are adaptable to changing business needs, especially in dynamic non-regulated markets.
*   **Capability Alignment:** Roles are designed to effectively execute specific business capabilities.
*   **Regulatory Separation:** Roles with direct responsibility for regulated functions are clearly delineated from those focused on non-regulated activities, with appropriate checks and balances.
