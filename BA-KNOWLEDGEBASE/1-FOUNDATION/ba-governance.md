# Business Architecture Governance
## Quantum Energy

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2025-11-25
**Scope:** Governance structure and processes for Business Architecture at Quantum Energy

---

## Executive Summary

This document establishes the governance framework for Business Architecture (BA) at Quantum Energy. It outlines the roles, responsibilities, committees, and processes necessary to ensure the effective creation, maintenance, and utilization of BA artifacts, supporting both our regulated utility obligations and our strategic non-regulated growth. The framework ensures strategic alignment, consistency, quality, and stakeholder engagement across our integrated enterprise.

---

## 1. Governance Objectives

The primary objectives of BA governance at Quantum Energy are to:

*   **Ensure Strategic Alignment:** Verify that BA efforts and artifacts directly support and enable Quantum Energy's strategic objectives, across regulated and non-regulated business lines.
*   **Maintain Consistency & Quality:** Establish standards and processes for the development and maintenance of BA artifacts, ensuring their accuracy, completeness, and adherence to BIZBOK and internal guidelines.
*   **Drive Value Realization:** Promote the effective use of BA artifacts to inform decision-making, prioritize investments, and guide transformation initiatives that deliver measurable value.
*   **Foster Collaboration & Communication:** Facilitate effective collaboration among stakeholders (business, IT, regulated, non-regulated, legal, etc.) and ensure clear communication of architectural decisions and their implications.
*   **Manage Change & Evolution:** Provide a structured process for managing changes to the business architecture, adapting to evolving strategies, market conditions, and regulatory environments.
*   **Ensure Regulatory Compliance & Separation:** Explicitly address the need to maintain regulatory compliance for utility operations and appropriate separation for non-regulated offerings within the architectural framework.

## 2. Governance Structure

The BA governance structure at Quantum Energy is multi-tiered, involving various committees and roles to ensure broad oversight and effective execution.

### 2.1. BA Steering Committee (BSC) - Executive Oversight

*   **Purpose:** Provide executive sponsorship and strategic direction for the Business Architecture practice. Ensure BA is aligned with overall enterprise strategy and investment priorities.
*   **Frequency:** Quarterly
*   **Membership:**
    *   Chief Strategy Officer (Chair)
    *   Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
    *   Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
    *   Chief Operating Officer (COO - Regulated Operations)
    *   Chief Digital & Innovation Officer (CDIO - Non-Regulated Business)
    *   Chief Information Officer (CIO)
    *   Chief Customer Officer (CCO)
    *   General Counsel
    *   VP, Regulatory Affairs
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Approve the BA strategy, roadmap, and annual work plan.
    *   Endorse major architectural changes and strategic initiatives.
    *   Resolve high-level conflicts or disputes regarding architectural decisions.
    *   Allocate significant resources for BA development and tool investments.
    *   Review BA value realization and impact on strategic objectives.
    *   Ensure appropriate governance regarding regulated/non-regulated boundaries.

### 2.2. BA Review Board (BARB) - Tactical Oversight & Quality Assurance

*   **Purpose:** Ensure the quality, consistency, and completeness of BA artifacts. Provide detailed review and approval of architectural changes and recommendations.
*   **Frequency:** Monthly
*   **Membership:**
    *   Chief Enterprise Architect / Head of Business Architecture (Chair)
    *   Lead Business Architects (Regulated, Non-Regulated, Enterprise)
    *   Enterprise Architecture Leads (Application, Data, Technology)
    *   Director, Strategic Planning
    *   Director, Portfolio Management Office (PMO)
    *   Key Business Unit (BU) Leadership (e.g., VP Electric Distribution, Head of Advanced Energy Solutions)
    *   Representatives from Legal & Regulatory as needed.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Review and approve new or significantly changed BA artifacts (e.g., capability models, value streams, product definitions).
    *   Ensure cross-domain consistency and adherence to BA standards and principles.
    *   Manage the architecture change request process.
    *   Prioritize BA development work and manage the BA backlog.
    *   Monitor BA usage and adoption across the organization.
    *   Address integration points and potential conflicts between regulated and non-regulated architectures.

### 2.3. BA Working Groups - Development & Maintenance

*   **Purpose:** Develop, maintain, and refine specific BA artifacts. Ensure subject matter expertise is integrated into the architecture.
*   **Frequency:** As needed (project or domain-specific)
*   **Membership:**
    *   Relevant Business Architects (Lead)
    *   Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) from business units (regulated, non-regulated).
    *   Representatives from IT, Process Excellence, Legal, Regulatory, Marketing, etc.
*   **Key Responsibilities:**
    *   Develop detailed BA artifacts (e.g., L3 capabilities, detailed value stream stages, information models).
    *   Conduct current state assessments and gather requirements.
    *   Perform gap analyses and define future state architectures.
    *   Ensure accuracy and relevance of artifacts.
    *   Propose changes and updates to the BARB.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

*   **Chief Enterprise Architect / Head of Business Architecture:**
    *   Define and champion the BA vision, strategy, and methodology.
    *   Lead the BA team and oversee all BA activities.
    *   Chair the BA Review Board.
    *   Report to the BA Steering Committee.
    *   Ensure adherence to governance processes and standards.
    *   Act as the primary point of contact for strategic BA initiatives.

*   **Lead Business Architect (Regulated Operations):**
    *   Accountable for the development and maintenance of BA artifacts specifically related to regulated utility operations (e.g., Electric/Gas Distribution, Energy Generation, Regulatory Compliance).
    *   Ensure architectural integrity and compliance with regulatory requirements.
    *   Collaborate with business unit leads and regulatory affairs.

*   **Lead Business Architect (Non-Regulated Offerings):**
    *   Accountable for the development and maintenance of BA artifacts for non-regulated business lines (e.g., Smart Home Solutions, EV Charging, Advanced Energy Solutions).
    *   Drive agile architecture development to support rapid market responsiveness and innovation.
    *   Collaborate with product development, marketing, and sales teams in non-regulated BUs.

*   **Enterprise Business Architect:**
    *   Develop cross-cutting BA artifacts and views (e.g., enterprise capability model, integrated stakeholder map, cross-segment value streams).
    *   Ensure consistency and integration across regulated and non-regulated architectural domains.
    *   Facilitate workshops and gather requirements from diverse stakeholders.

*   **Business Stakeholders / Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):**
    *   Provide expertise, validate architectural models, and participate in working groups.
    *   Serve as consumers and advocates for BA artifacts.
    *   Articulate business needs and strategic priorities.

*   **IT & Other Architecture Domains:**
    *   Collaborate with Business Architecture to ensure alignment between business and technology architectures.
    *   Provide technical feasibility and impact assessments.

## 4. Architecture Artifact Lifecycle Management

### 4.1. Creation & Development

1.  **Initiation:** A new BA artifact or significant change is proposed (e.g., new strategic initiative, new product offering, regulatory change).
2.  **Scope & Planning:** Business Architect, in collaboration with stakeholders, defines the scope, objectives, and deliverables.
3.  **Development:** Business Architect(s) and Working Group develop the artifact, adhering to BA standards and principles.
4.  **Internal Review:** Artifact undergoes peer review within the BA team.

### 4.2. Review & Approval

1.  **BARB Review:** Developed artifact is presented to the BA Review Board for detailed review, feedback, and approval. Focus on quality, consistency, and alignment.
2.  **BSC Endorsement (Strategic Only):** For major enterprise-level artifacts or significant strategic shifts, endorsement by the BA Steering Committee is required after BARB approval.
3.  **Publication:** Approved artifact is published to the central BA Knowledgebase.

### 4.3. Maintenance & Evolution

1.  **Regular Review Cycles:** Each artifact has a defined review frequency (e.g., annual for L1/L2 capabilities, quarterly for product catalogs).
2.  **Change Requests:** Stakeholders can submit change requests for any BA artifact.
3.  **Impact Assessment:** Business Architect assesses the impact of proposed changes across related artifacts and domains, including regulated/non-regulated implications.
4.  **Update & Re-approval:** Changes are implemented, reviewed, and re-approved through the BARB (and potentially BSC for major changes).

## 5. Standards and Tools

*   **Modeling Standards:** Adherence to BIZBOK metamodel principles and notations.
*   **Documentation Standards:** Use of Markdown for textual artifacts. Standard templates for consistency.
*   **Naming Conventions:** Clear and consistent naming conventions for artifacts and elements (e.g., `reg-`, `nonreg-`, `ent-` prefixes).
*   **Version Control:** Git repository for all BA artifacts.
*   **Collaboration Platform:** (e.g., Confluence, SharePoint) for stakeholder communication and access to published artifacts.
*   **Modeling Tools:** (e.g., Draw.io/Lucidchart for diagrams; potential future dedicated BA tool).

---

## 6. Communication Plan

Effective communication is critical for the success and adoption of Business Architecture at Quantum Energy.

*   **BA Knowledgebase:** Central repository for all published artifacts.
*   **Executive Briefings:** Quarterly updates to the BA Steering Committee and broader executive leadership.
*   **Stakeholder Workshops:** Regular sessions to gather input, validate models, and communicate key architectural decisions.
*   **Internal Newsletter/Intranet:** Highlight BA achievements, updates, and upcoming events.
*   **Training & Awareness:** Provide training on BA concepts, methodologies, and how to utilize BA artifacts.

---

## 7. Conflict Resolution

Conflicts arising from architectural decisions or interpretations will be escalated through the governance structure:

1.  **Working Group Resolution:** Attempted at the working group level.
2.  **BARB Resolution:** If unresolved, escalated to the BA Review Board.
3.  **BSC Resolution:** For major strategic impacts or cross-BU disagreements, escalated to the BA Steering Committee.
