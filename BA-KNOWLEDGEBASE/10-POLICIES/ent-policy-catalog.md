## Executive Summary

This document provides a high-level catalog of Quantum Energy's key policies, regulations, and business rules. These directives govern the behavior of the organization, ensuring ethical conduct, legal and regulatory compliance, and consistent operations across all business segments. The catalog differentiates between policies primarily impacting regulated utility operations, non-regulated advanced energy solutions, and enterprise-wide functions, highlighting the complex compliance landscape we navigate.

---

## 1. Introduction

Policies and rules are formal statements that guide decision-making and actions within Quantum Energy. They ensure that our operations align with legal requirements, ethical standards, and our strategic objectives, while protecting our customers, employees, and assets.

## 2. Policy Categories & Descriptions

Quantum Energy's policies and rules are broadly categorized as follows:

### 2.1. Regulatory Policies (Primarily Regulated)

*   **Description:** External rules, laws, and mandates imposed by governmental or quasi-governmental agencies that directly govern Quantum Energy's regulated electric and natural gas utility operations. Non-compliance carries legal and financial penalties, and can impact our license to operate.
*   **Key Examples:**
    *   **Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) Orders:** Governing interstate transmission, wholesale markets.
    *   **State Public Utility Commission (PUC) Orders:** Governing rates, service quality, customer protection, capital investments.
    *   **North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC) Reliability Standards:** Ensuring bulk electric system reliability.
    *   **Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA) Regulations:** Governing natural gas pipeline safety.
    *   **Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Regulations:** Governing air, water, and waste emissions from power plants and operations.
    *   **Customer Service & Billing Rules:** Mandates regarding service turn-on/off, billing formats, collections.
*   **Source:** External regulatory bodies.
*   **Impact:** Directly impacts Regulated Operations, Rate Cases, Compliance & Reporting.

### 2.2. Corporate Policies (Enterprise-wide)

*   **Description:** Internal directives established by Quantum Energy's leadership to govern the overall conduct, operations, and ethics of the company. These apply to all employees and business units (regulated and non-regulated).
*   **Key Examples:**
    *   **Code of Business Conduct and Ethics:** Guiding employee behavior, conflicts of interest.
    *   **Cybersecurity Policy:** Protecting all enterprise information and operational technology assets.
    *   **Data Privacy Policy:** Governing collection, use, and protection of all customer and employee data (including non-regulated product data).
    *   **Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S) Policy:** Ensuring safe working conditions and environmental stewardship.
    *   **Procurement Policy:** Governing vendor selection, contracting, and supply chain management.
    *   **Information Security Policy:** Protecting information assets from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
    *   **Human Resources Policies:** Governing employment, compensation, benefits, diversity, equity, and inclusion.
    *   **Investment Approval Policy:** Criteria and process for approving capital and strategic investments.
*   **Source:** Internal (Board, Executive Leadership).
*   **Impact:** Enterprise-wide, covers all business units.

### 2.3. Operational Policies & Procedures (Primarily Regulated, some Non-Regulated)

*   **Description:** Detailed internal rules and step-by-step instructions that govern specific operational activities within Quantum Energy. These ensure consistency, efficiency, and safety in day-to-day work.
*   **Key Examples:**
    *   **Electric Grid Switching Procedures:** Step-by-step for operating power lines and equipment.
    *   **Gas Pipeline Excavation Procedures:** Safety protocols for digging near gas lines.
    *   **Outage Restoration Priorities:** Rules for prioritizing service restoration during emergencies.
    *   **Asset Maintenance Procedures:** Protocols for inspecting, maintaining, and repairing assets.
    *   **Non-Regulated Product Installation Procedures:** Standards for installing smart home devices, EV chargers.
    *   **Non-Regulated Technical Support Procedures:** Guidelines for handling customer inquiries for AES products.
*   **Source:** Internal (Operational Leadership).
*   **Impact:** Specific operational units within Regulated Operations or AES.

### 2.4. Business Rules (Enterprise-wide, often product-specific)

*   **Description:** Specific statements that define or constrain aspects of the business, dictating how data is processed or decisions are made. They are often embedded in systems and influence product behavior.
*   **Key Examples:**
    *   **Credit & Deposit Requirements:** Criteria for requiring a security deposit from a new regulated customer.
    *   **Disconnection/Reconnection Criteria:** Rules for terminating or reinstating utility service for non-payment.
    *   **Rate Application Logic:** Algorithms for applying tariffs to consumption data for billing.
    *   **Non-Regulated Product Eligibility Rules:** Criteria for customer qualification for smart home programs.
    *   **Non-Regulated Pricing Rules:** Logic for dynamic pricing or discount application for AES offerings.
    *   **Payment Plan Eligibility:** Rules for offering budget billing or payment arrangements.
*   **Source:** Business Owners, Product Managers.
*   **Impact:** Cross-functional, impacts various systems (billing, CRM, non-regulated platforms).

### 2.5. Tariffs & Service Terms (Regulated & Non-Regulated)

*   **Description:** Formal documents defining the terms, conditions, and prices for services provided by Quantum Energy. Tariffs are regulated, while non-regulated service terms are contractual agreements.
*   **Key Examples:**
    *   **Regulated Tariffs:** Approved rate schedules for electric and natural gas services, defining charges, riders, and service rules.
    *   **Non-Regulated Service Terms & Conditions:** Legal agreements outlining the terms for non-regulated products/services (e.g., smart home subscription agreements, EV charging network terms).
    *   **Interconnection Agreements:** Terms for connecting customer-owned generation (DERs) to the grid.
*   **Source:** Regulated (PUC approval); Non-Regulated (AES Legal).
*   **Impact:** Directly impacts billing, customer contracts, sales, and product delivery.

## 3. Governance & Management

Quantum Energy manages its policies and rules through a structured governance framework:
*   **Policy Ownership:** Each policy has a designated owner responsible for its development, review, and maintenance.
*   **Review & Approval:** Policies are reviewed and approved by relevant committees (e.g., Legal, Regulatory, ELT) based on their scope and impact.
*   **Centralized Repository:** All approved policies and rules are maintained in a central, accessible repository.
*   **Communication & Training:** Policies are effectively communicated to all affected employees, and relevant training is provided.
*   **Regular Audits:** Periodic audits ensure adherence to policies and identify areas for improvement.
