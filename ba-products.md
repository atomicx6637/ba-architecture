# Customer Product Catalog
## Vertically Integrated Natural Gas and Electric Utility

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2025-11-25
**Standard:** BIZBOK Guide to Business Architecture
**Scope:** Complete customer-facing product portfolio

---

## Executive Summary

This document defines the comprehensive product catalog for a vertically integrated natural gas and electric utility serving residential, commercial, and industrial customer segments. Products are organized by category and represent the complete set of offerings delivered to customers.

In BIZBOK terminology, **products** are tangible or intangible goods and services that the organization offers to stakeholders (primarily customers) to fulfill value propositions. This catalog maps to:
- **Capabilities:** Products are delivered through business capabilities
- **Value Streams:** Products are the outcomes of value stream execution
- **Stakeholders:** Products serve specific customer segments and stakeholder needs
- **Information:** Products generate transaction and usage data

---

## Product Catalog Structure

The product catalog is organized into seven primary categories:

1. **Core Electric Energy Products**
2. **Core Natural Gas Energy Products**
3. **Dual-Fuel Products**
4. **Customer Services and Programs**
5. **Distributed Energy Resource (DER) Products**
6. **Commercial and Industrial Solutions**
7. **Ancillary and Value-Added Services**

Each product includes:
- Product name and identifier
- Description and value proposition
- Target customer segments
- Delivery model
- Enabling capabilities

**Description:** Programs allowing gas customers to support renewable natural gas (biogas) in the gas supply portfolio.

**Value Proposition:** Environmental benefits through support of renewable gas sources with similar delivery characteristics to conventional natural gas.

**Customer Segments:**
- Residential gas customers
- Commercial gas customers
- Institutional customers with sustainability goals

**Service Components:**
- RNG allocation to customer account
- Environmental attribute certification
- Carbon intensity reporting
- Renewable gas supply premium billing

**Delivery Model:** Subscription programs with monthly premium charges

**Enabling L1 Capabilities:**
- Natural Gas Supply
- Customer Engagement and Service
- Billing and Revenue Management

## Product Lifecycle Considerations

**New Product Development Process:**
1. Market analysis and customer needs assessment
2. Value proposition and business case development
3. Regulatory approval and tariff filing (where required)
4. Product design and capability readiness
5. Pilot testing and refinement
6. Full market launch and scaling
7. Ongoing performance monitoring and optimization

**Product Retirement Criteria:**
- Low customer adoption and participation
- Regulatory or policy changes
- Technology obsolescence
- Portfolio rationalization
- Cost-to-serve exceeds value delivered
